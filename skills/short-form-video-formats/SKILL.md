# Short-Form Video Formats

A reference skill for planning, formatting, and scripting short-form videos (such as TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts) based on three fundamental content pillars: **Yap**, **Educational**, and **Storytelling**.

---

## Video Formats Comparison

Use the following table to identify the right format based on the topic, desired length, and posting cadence:

| Format | Spoken Duration | Filming Styles | Core Topic Areas | Posting Frequency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yap** | 15–45 seconds | Natural (multitasking, talking form, green screen) | Hot takes, opinions, unique perspectives, casual advice | 1–2 times per week |
| **Educational** | 30–60 seconds | Unnatural (visuals, clones, back-and-forth, split screen, Q&A) | Tutorials, tips/hacks, do's & don'ts, myth busting, comparisons | 2–3 times per week |
| **Storytelling** | 45–90 seconds | Voiceover, audio + B-roll + text overlays | Personal/business stories, wins, losses, experiences, lessons | 1–2 times per week |

---

## 1. The Yap Format (Natural & Conversation-first)

**Yap** videos are designed to feel unscripted, raw, and highly conversational. The goal is to build a personal connection and showcase authentic personality.

### Key Characteristics
- **Duration:** 15–45 seconds.
- **Filming Style:** Casual, natural formats. Often filmed while doing something else (multitasking like getting ready or walking) or as a direct "talking head" to the camera, occasionally utilizing a green screen.
- **Content Topics:** Hot takes, opinions, unique perspectives, or casual, friendly advice ("bestie" style).
- **Posting Cadence:** 1–2 times per week.

### Scripting Guidelines & Template
Keep the script extremely casual. Avoid stiff transitions or formal language. Use fillers like "literally," "honestly," or "here's the thing" to mimic natural speech.

```
[Hook - Bold/Opinionated Take]
"Honestly, if you are still [common practice], you are [result/pain point]. Here is why..."

[Perspective/Advice - Explaining the Take]
"I was talking to a friend about this, and [personal anecdote or observation]. Everyone is trying to [popular trend], but in reality, [your unique perspective/bestie advice]."

[CTA - Engaging the Audience]
"What do you think? Am I tripping or is this actually true? Let me know."
```

## 2. The Educational Format (Highly Structured & Edited)

**Educational** videos focus on delivering quick, high-value information using engaging visual formats. The goal is to establish authority and provide immediate utility.

### Key Characteristics
- **Duration:** 30–60 seconds.
- **Filming Style:** Unnatural, highly produced formats. Think visual clones (the creator talking to themselves), green screens, split screens, quick-cut Q&As, and graphic animations.
- **Content Topics:** Step-by-step tutorials, tips & hacks, do's vs. don'ts, myth busting, common mistakes, or comparison of options.
- **Posting Cadence:** 2–3 times per week.

### Scripting Guidelines & Template
Deliver value immediately. Cut out the fluff. Use text overlays to highlight key points.

```
[Hook - Problem/Hack Callout]
"This one [niche] hack will save you hours of [task]. Watch this."

[Step-by-Step Breakdown]
"[Visual/Clone Action: Show step 1]
First, you're going to [action]. Don't do [common mistake], do [correct method] instead.

[Visual/Split-Screen: Show step 2]
Second, set up [configuration]. This is how you [benefit/result]."

[Quick Myth Bust / Contrast]
"Most people think [myth], but [fact]."

[CTA - Saved Content / Lead Magnet]
"Save this video for the next time you need to [task]."
```

## 3. The Storytelling Format (Narrative-driven)

**Storytelling** videos connect with the audience on an emotional level by sharing journeys, vulnerability, and lessons. The goal is to drive deep brand loyalty and trust.

### Key Characteristics
- **Duration:** 45–90 seconds.
- **Filming Style:** Voiceover audio recorded separately, overlaid with high-quality B-roll footage and large caption text overlays.
- **Content Topics:** Founder stories, business origins, major wins, losses, experiences, lessons learned, and transformations.
- **Posting Cadence:** 1–2 times per week.

### Scripting Guidelines & Template
Follow a standard narrative arc: Setup -> Conflict -> Resolution/Transformation -> Lesson.

```
[Hook - The Peak Conflict/Starting Point]
"In [Year], we were [extreme situation/loss]. I had no idea how we'd survive."

[The Journey/Conflict]
"Every day felt like [vivid description of effort]. We tried [strategy A] and failed. We almost [quitting moment]."

[The Resolution/Transformation]
"But then, we shifted our focus to [breakthrough action]. Slowly, things started changing. [Specific metric of win/transformation]."

[The Key Lesson]
"If there's one thing I learned from this, it's [key lesson]."

[CTA - Soft Brand Connection]
"Follow to see how we build this from here."
```

## How to Apply Video Formats

When asked to generate video concepts or scripts:

1. **Classify the Request:** Determine if the goal is to build connection (**Yap**), teach a skill (**Educational**), or inspire (**Storytelling**).
2. **Select Format & Style:** Apply the corresponding duration, filming layout recommendation, and pacing rules.
3. **Format Scripts for Short-form Editing:** For all generated scripts, format output with distinct sections:
   - **Filming Setup / Format Type**
   - **On-Camera Visuals / B-roll instructions**
   - **Spoken Audio / Voiceover text**
   - **On-Screen Text Overlays**
4. **Mix with Structures:** Combine these categories with templates from [short-form-content-structures](file:///Users/deakdavid/Documents/Portfolio/Social-Media-Content-Creation-Skill/skills/short-form-content-structures/SKILL.md) (e.g., using a *Countdown* structure for an **Educational** video, or a *Nostalgic Remember* hook for a **Storytelling** video).
