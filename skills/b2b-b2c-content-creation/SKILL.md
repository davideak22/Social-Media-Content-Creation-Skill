# B2B vs B2C Content Creation

A reference skill for distinguishing, structuring, and generating social media content tailored to B2B (Business-to-Business) and B2C (Business-to-Consumer) audiences. This skill helps you craft hooks, scripts, and prompts that align with the decision-making psychology of each audience type.

---

## B2B vs B2C Dynamics Comparison

| Aspect | B2B (Business-to-Business) | B2C (Business-to-Consumer) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core Message Focus** | Social proof, authority, results, competence | Broad, fun, relatable, lifestyle-oriented |
| **Primary Driver** | Logical certainty, ROI, risk reduction, credibility | Emotional connection, entertainment, impulse |
| **Target Audience** | Multiple decision-makers (managers, executives, teams) | Single decision-maker (the consumer) |
| **Desired Outcome** | Establish authority so they trust your expertise | Build familiarity and trigger immediate emotional action |

---

## Case Study: The Dual-Contrast Method

One of the most effective ways to highlight B2B vs B2C dynamics on camera is a **Dual-Contrast Script** where you act out or compare the two styles side-by-side. This drives high engagement because it educates and entertains at the same time.

### Reference Script: B2B vs B2C Comparison

Below is the transcript structure of a high-performing comparison video showing the difference between B2B and B2C content:

- **Filming Setup:** Split screen or quick-cut character swaps (same creator, different outfits/postures).
- **On-Camera Visuals:** Fast character changes with clear labels ("B2C Content" vs. "B2B Content").

```
[00:00:00 - Character A: Casual, expressive]
On-Screen Text: B2C Content
"This is content for B2C: Unboxing time! Oh my god, these cloud slides are literally like walking on marshmallows..."

[00:00:04 - Character B: Professional, direct]
On-Screen Text: B2B Content
"This is content for B2B: How we scaled our client's SaaS from zero to forty-thousand dollars in monthly recurring revenue."

[00:00:08 - Character A: Casual, friendly]
On-Screen Text: B2C Content
"This is content for B2C: Tell me I'm not the only one who does a whole deep-clean of the kitchen instead of studying..."

[00:00:12 - Character B: Direct, authoritative]
On-Screen Text: B2B Content
"This is content for B2B: If you aren't mapping your churn rate, you have no idea how much money is leaking from your business."

[00:00:18 - Breakdown/Explanation: Authoritative tone]
On-Screen Text: The Real Difference
"See the difference? B2B content focuses on systems, credibility, and real business outcomes. B2C content relies on lifestyle, emotional relatability, and impulse."

[00:00:26 - Key Psychology Takeaway]
"B2B is more complex and involves a lot of decision-makers who need certainty and proof that you know what you're doing. B2C is usually one decision-maker and is usually an impulse driven by emotion."

[00:00:36 - CTA with Keyword Comment Hook]
On-Screen Text: Comment "IDEAS" For Prompts
"Now, if you want the complete list of B2B and B2C prompts to map out a whole month of content ideas, comment 'IDEAS' and I'll send it over."
```

## AI Prompts for Content Ideation

Use these prompts to generate months of niche-specific content ideas:

### B2B Idea Generator Prompt

```
Act as a B2B organic growth expert. I want you to brainstorm a list of 15 short-form video concepts (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) for a [insert product/niche/service]. 
Each concept must prioritize establishing:
1. Authority & Expertise (e.g., explaining complex funnels, industry systems)
2. Social Proof & Credibility (e.g., business milestones, case studies)
3. Results & ROI (e.g., how to save/make money or save time)

For each concept, provide:
- A hook matching this criteria
- A brief outline of the value delivery
- A CTA prompting the user to download a resource or leave a comment
```

### B2C Idea Generator Prompt

```
Act as a B2C viral marketing specialist. I want you to brainstorm a list of 15 short-form video concepts (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) for [insert product/niche]. 
Each concept must prioritize:
1. Broad relatability and everyday humor/scenarios
2. Emotional connection (e.g., excitement, nostalgia, frustration)
3. High entertainment value (e.g., aesthetic routines, fun reactions)

For each concept, provide:
- A scroll-stopping emotional hook
- A quick narrative/entertainment breakdown
- An interactive CTA (e.g., "tag a friend," "save this for later")
```

## How to Generate B2B or B2C Scripts

When generating scripts for a user:
1. **Identify the Business Model:** Ask or determine if their product/niche targets businesses (B2B) or consumers (B2C).
2. **Apply the Right Tone and Hooks:**
   - For B2B, use hook formulas that promise results, showcase case studies, or point out funnel inefficiencies.
   - For B2C, use hooks focused on relatability, fun, routine, or immediate emotion.
3. **Structure CTAs Appropriately:**
   - B2B CTAs should lead to high-value lead magnets, guides, or direct comment triggers (e.g., *Comment "SYSTEM" to get the template*).
   - B2C CTAs should invite comments, likes, saves, or quick emotional interactions.
