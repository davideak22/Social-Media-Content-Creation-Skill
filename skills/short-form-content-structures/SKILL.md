# Short-Form Content Structures

A reference skill for generating short-form video scripts and content ideas using 10 proven viral structures. Use these as fill-in-the-blank templates — slot in the creator's niche or topic and the structure does the heavy lifting.

---

## The 10 Structures

### 1. Insert Blank Maxing

**Format:** `[Topic] maxing 101`
**Example:** `Energy drink maxing 101`
Best for lifestyle, productivity, wellness, or hobby niches. Implies expertise and community identity.

---

### 2. My Top 10 (Countdown)

**Format:** `My top 10. 10, 9, 8...`
**Example:** `My top 10 productivity apps. 10, 9, 8...`
Rapid-fire list. Works in any niche. High retention because viewers want to see what #1 is.

---

### 3. The Remember Structure

**Format:** `Remember [nostalgic or relatable detail]?`
**Example:** `Remember? Hoodie, messy bun, coffee at 2am.`
Triggers familiarity and emotion. Works great for lifestyle, student life, or throwback content.

---

### 4. 10 Out of 10 Insert Blank (Increasingly Niche)

**Format:** `10 out of 10 signs that [X], but it gets increasingly more niche`
**Example:** `10 out of 10 signs the person you're talking to isn't as smart as you think — but it gets increasingly more niche`
Comedy and relatability. Works because the niche escalation is a payoff viewers stay for.

---

### 5. Peak X Life

**Format:** `Peak [identity/lifestyle] life: [list of specific relatable details]`
**Example:** `Peak unbothered life: no car payment, never checking prices when you go out to eat`
Strong identity-based content. The more specific and aspirational the details, the better.

---

### 6. Once a Day / Week / Month (or Year)

**Format:** `Once a day, once a week, once a month: [habit or routine]`
Best for wellness, habits, self-improvement, or productivity content. Clean and easy to follow.

---

### 7. Bracket Battles

**Format:** `[Option A] or [Option B]? [Creator picks]`
**Example:** `Dior or Chanel? Dior.`
Fast, punchy, opinionated. Great for fashion, food, lifestyle. Drives comments and debate.

---

### 8. The Hand Wave (Attention Reset)

**Format:** Start with a hand wave or visual cue, rapid-fire list follows (Breakfast, lunch, dinner...)
Works as a pattern interrupt. Resets viewer attention mid-scroll. Pair with a fast-cut edit style.

---

### 9. Your Attention Span Is Fried

**Format:** `Your attention span is fried. You don't need a niche. You have many interests.`
Meta-commentary on the viewer themselves. Works well as a hook or transition to a list.

---

### 10. List With Fingers

**Format:** Count off items using your fingers on camera while narrating
**Example:** `No one talks about this. You're not going to like this, but...` (then count on fingers)
Visual and tactile. Keeps the camera dynamic. Works for any list-based content.

---

## Usage Notes

These structures work best when mixed with other content types. Don't only post structured videos. Balance them with:

- Yap-style videos (unscripted talking to camera)
- Storytelling videos
- Educational step-by-step tutorials

Structures are a high-efficiency format when you're busy and need to post while still demonstrating authority in your niche. They are not a replacement for personality-driven content.

---

## How to Apply a Structure

When given a topic or niche, choose the best-fit structure and output a ready-to-film script:

1. Identify the niche and desired tone (educational, aspirational, comedic, relatable)
2. Match the niche to one or more structures above
3. Fill in the template with specific, niche-relevant details
4. Keep the total video under 60 seconds in spoken length
5. Suggest a hook line and a CTA (call to action) at the end if needed
