# Short-Form Content Maxing

A reference skill for optimizing short-form content creation (TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts) based on the principles of organic audience building, content allocation ratios, and personal branding fundamentals.

---

## The Personal Brand Equation

To build a loyal following that likes you and values whatever you post, balance educational value with personal storytelling and transparent documentation:

$$\text{Expert Education Content} + \text{Personal Storytelling / Documentation} = \text{Personal Brand}$$

### Key Branding Dynamics
- **Audience Connection:** A true personal brand following likes you as an individual. Once this bond is established, they will engage with whatever you post because they trust your voice.
- **Vulnerability & Authority:** Mix high-authority tutorials with raw behind-the-scenes moments, lessons from failures, and day-to-day documentation.
- **Patience:** Building a personal brand takes years of consistent daily execution.

---

## The 70-20-10 Content Allocation Rule

To optimize reach and growth, distribute your video topics using the **70-20-10 Content Ratio** (credited to Caleb Ralston):

| Percentage | Category | Action | Method / Execution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **70%** | Virally Proven | Target proven niche topics | Transcribe viral video scripts from online, convert them to fill-in-the-blank structures, use proven hooks, and adapt them to your topic. |
| **20%** | Outlier Double-Down | Recreate your own viral videos | When a video performs well on your account, recreate it over and over. Rotate formats, iterate on the hooks, and expand on the value. |
| **10%** | New Ideas / Trends | Experiment with new concepts | Test completely new formats, untested concepts, or unique trends to discover new viral outliers. |

## Short-Form Content Maxing Principles

When planning and filming content, keep these execution guidelines in mind:

### 1. Idea is King
- The concept, value proposition, and topic hook dictate 90% of the video's performance.
- A video can be filmed very simply or boringly, but if the core idea is strong, it will perform. Do not let high production value substitute for a weak idea.

### 2. Learn to Post When Busy
- Content creation is a long-term reputation asset. You only have one reputation—never compromise the value of your output.
- Build systems to write, film, and schedule content efficiently so you can publish consistently even on your busiest days.

### 3. Maintain Consistency
- Commit to posting every day. Success in short-form content takes years of compounding daily effort.
- Embrace trends, but keep trying and iterating even when you feel like you aren't good at a specific trend.

## Content Maxing Application Strategy

When helping a user plan their content strategy or brainstorm scripts:

1. **Audit Niche Outliers:** Look for competitor content to fulfill the **70% Virally Proven** allocation.
2. **Double Down on Success:** Identify the user's past top-performing videos to fulfill the **20% Outlier Double-Down** allocation.
3. **Draft High-Concept Hooks:** Make sure every script starts with a proven verbal and visual pattern interrupt (e.g. `Explaining [Topic] So a 5 Year old Could Understand`).
4. **Determine the Filming Format:** Map the topic to the correct format (Yap, Educational, or Storytelling) using [short-form-video-formats](file:///Users/deakdavid/Documents/Portfolio/Social-Media-Content-Creation-Skill/skills/short-form-video-formats/SKILL.md).
